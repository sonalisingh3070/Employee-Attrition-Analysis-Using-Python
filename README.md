# Employee Attrition Analysis Using Python
## Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition using Python to identify the factors that influence employee turnover. The analysis 
includes data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and visualizations to uncover patterns related to employee attrition.
Based on the findings, business insights and recommendations are provided to help improve employee retention.
## Objectives
- Analyze employee attrition trends.
- Identify factors affecting employee turnover.
- Explore relationships between attrition and employee characteristics.
- Generate actionable business insights.
- Recommend strategies to improve employee retention.
## Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Jupyter Notebook
## Dataset
- Dataset: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
- Records: 1,470 employees
- Features: 35 columns
## Key Insights
- Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition.
- Lower job satisfaction is associated with increased employee turnover.
- Lower-income employees are more likely to leave the organization.
- Employees with fewer years at the company have a higher attrition rate.
- Certain departments and job roles experience higher attrition than others.
## Business Recommendations
- Reduce excessive overtime through better workload planning.
- Improve employee engagement and job satisfaction.
- Provide competitive compensation and career growth opportunities.
- Strengthen onboarding and retention programs for new employees.
- Conduct regular employee feedback surveys to identify retention risks early.
